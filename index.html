Nasdaq income scenario simulator

Set a Nasdaq-100 cumulative return and volatility, then estimate QQQ, GPIQ and QQQH outcomes across many paths ending at that exact return. The model reports cash distributions separately from ending capital so yield is not double-counted.

Scenario inputs

Every simulated Nasdaq path finishes at this exact cumulative return.
Volatility changes the path, which matters materially for QQQH’s monthly put-spread collar.

Cash payout assumptions

QQQH’s distribution rate is a cash-payout rate, not a forecast return. Payments may include option premium, gains or return of capital.
Advanced return assumptions
QQQH does not publish fixed strikes. The call cap and put triggers are illustrative and editable; the fund may close options early.
Median cash distributions Income taken as cash, not reinvested
Median investment path Total-return value before tax
Outcome detail 10th–90th percentile reflects path uncertainty, not forecast confidence
Do not interpret the payout rate as extra return. A fund can pay a high distribution while its NAV falls. “Total wealth” below equals ending capital plus cash received. Reinvested value is calculated separately.

GPIQ: positive and negative monthly Nasdaq returns are multiplied by editable upside/downside capture assumptions. The default 10.5% monthly-paid distribution is split out of that total return rather than added to it.

QQQH: each simulated month is passed through an editable put-spread collar: upside is limited by a call cap; losses are flattened between the long-put and short-put triggers; losses resume below the short-put strike with the spread width retained. An editable net option credit and the 0.68% expense ratio are then applied. The fixed strikes are illustrative because QQQH actively manages and may close options before expiry.

Sources for defaults: Goldman Sachs reported a 10.5% annualized GPIQ distribution rate and 84.86% upside / 65.72% downside capture in its May 2026 fund update. NEOS reported a 9.27% QQQH distribution rate and 0.68% expense ratio as of 30 June 2026. QQQH’s prospectus describes full or sampled Nasdaq-100 exposure plus a monthly put-spread collar using sold calls, purchased puts and sold lower-strike puts; options typically begin with about six weeks to expiry and are rolled at month-end. See Goldman Sachs, NEOS QQQH and the QQQH prospectus.

This is a simplified educational model, not a forecast or tax calculation. It ignores brokerage, withholding tax, currency movements and individual tax treatment.
